# Provider Re-credentialing Process

## Overview

The re-credentialing process ensures healthcare providers maintain current and valid credentials in the Provider Directory. It runs on a 3-year cycle as required by NCQA (National Committee for Quality Assurance) standards and is managed by the Provider Network team.

## Details

Required every 3 years per NCQA standards to maintain provider credentials in the Provider Directory. The Provider Network team manages this process, which involves verifying provider demographics, credentials, board certifications, practice locations, and service areas. Ensures that only properly credentialed providers remain in-network and that their information in the Provider Directory remains current and accurate. Failure to complete re-credentialing may result in network termination.
